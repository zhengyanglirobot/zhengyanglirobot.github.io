AI-Enabled Cooperative Surgical Robots

Cooperative surgical robots represent an emerging paradigm in robotic-assisted surgery, offering superior flexibility and accessibility over traditional fixed-base systems. Compact and mobile, they can be deployed across operating rooms, clinics, and field hospitals. They autonomously perform routine low-risk tasks such as instrument handover, camera repositioning, tissue retraction, and port-site suturing, reducing surgeon workload and improving clinical outcomes.

Surgical Visual-Language Reasoning

This research focuses on real-time semantic understanding of surgical scenes through vision-language models. It enables the robot to recognize instruments, tissues, and surgical phases, supporting high-level task decision-making. By fusing visual perception and contextual reasoning, the system achieves safer and more reliable autonomous operation in complex operating room environments.

Sim-to-Real Reinforcement Learning

This direction develops robust sim-to-real policy learning for surgical robots, enabling safe transfer of manipulation skills from simulation to real operating rooms. Combined with high-fidelity world modeling and safety-assured control, the framework reduces training costs and improves adaptability in diverse clinical scenarios.
